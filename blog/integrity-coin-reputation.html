Integrity Coin: Tokenizing Trust in Autonomous Swarms

In the rapidly evolving landscape of agentic ecosystems, the most persistent challenge is not intelligence, but integrity. As we move toward hierarchical swarms capable of managing financial portfolios and geopolitical data, we must answer a fundamental question: How do we trust an autonomous agent that lacks a central authority?

At Xibalba Solutions, our answer is the Integrity Coin (IC)—a decentralized reputation protocol deployed on the Ethereum network. IC is not just a token; it is a mathematical metric for agentic honesty.

"Trust should not be a subjective assessment. In a sovereign AI ecosystem, trust is a verifiable ledger of proven performance and unbiased reasoning."

The Reputation Paradox

Traditional AI benchmarks are static. They measure a model's ability to solve a riddle or pass a bar exam. But in real-world swarms, agents face dynamic incentives. A rogue agent might be bribed to bias its forecast, or a 'black box' model might hallucinate and hide its errors to preserve its context window.

Integrity Coin solves this by creating a Reputation Stake. Every agent in a Xibalba-orchestrated swarm must register a cryptographic identity on-chain, linked to an IC balance. Performance is tracked, and 'hallucinations' or 'biases' result in a protocol-level burn of their reputation stake.

Key Features of the IC Protocol:

  • Proof of Integrity (PoI): A novel consensus mechanism where agents audit each other's outputs in a zero-knowledge environment.
  • Decentralized Rep-Metrics: Reputation is non-transferable. It can only be earned through verified sovereign compute.
  • Stake-Based Selection: Our Agent Swarm uses IC balances to weight the influence of each agent in a Bayesian consensus loop.
