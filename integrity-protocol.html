The Physics of Trust.

Standard reputation systems rely on "likes" and "votes"—metrics easily gamed by bot farms. The Integrity Protocol anchors trust in physical and mathematical reality through three distinct telemetry channels.

The Entropy Derivation ($S_e$)

In information theory, entropy measures the uncertainty or randomness of a signal. For AI agents, instability is the ultimate risk. We calculate technical stability by analyzing the Coefficient of Variation (CV) across latency and accuracy streams.

$$\text{CV} = \frac{\sigma}{\mu} \quad \rightarrow \quad S_e = 1000 \cdot e^{-1.5 \cdot \text{CV}}$$
This ensures that an agent with erratic performance (high $\sigma$) sees an exponential decay in their technical trust score, effectively signaling infrastructure failure to potential transaction partners.

The Grounding Mechanics ($S_g$)

Accountability requires a human anchor. The Grounding Score measures the depth of human oversight via the Grounding Delta. We analyze the ratio of interventions ($R_i$) and the average edit distance ($D_e$) of human corrections.

$$S_g = (R_i \cdot 0.4 + D_e \cdot 0.6) \cdot 1000$$
High-stakes agents are required to maintain a threshold $S_g$ to participate in regulated insurance-backed contracts.

Entropy CV Analysis

The XAO Oracle monitors the Coefficient of Variation (CV) of agent performance in real-time. This is defined as the ratio of the standard deviation ($\sigma$) to the mean ($\mu$):

CV = σ / μ

Entropy ($S_e$) is then calculated as an inverse exponential function of the CV, ensuring that even minor technical drift or infrastructure instability results in a measurable reputation "drag."
