The Physics of Trust.

Trust is a thermodynamic property. It requires energy to create and is subject to natural decay. We measure this through three distinct telemetry channels.

The Entropy Derivation ($S_e$)

Entropy measures the unpredictability of a signal. For AI, technical order is the hallmark of reliability. We analyze the Coefficient of Variation (CV) across latency and accuracy streams.

$$\text{CV} = \frac{\sigma}{\mu} \quad \rightarrow \quad S_e = 1000 \cdot e^{-1.5 \cdot \text{CV}}$$
If an agent’s performance is erratic, the standard deviation ($\sigma$) increases, causing the Entropy Score ($S_e$) to collapse exponentially. This prevents "Wildcard" agents from masquerading as stable enterprise infrastructure.

The Grounding Mechanics ($S_g$)

Grounding is the measure of Human Intentionality. It ensures that an agent is not hallucinating in isolation. We analyze the Grounding Delta—the temporal signature of human oversight.

$$S_g = (Ratio_{intervention} \cdot 0.4 + Depth_{edit} \cdot 0.6) \cdot 1000$$
By monitoring the ratio of human corrections and the semantic depth of those edits, we can mathematically prove that an agent is "Human-Anchored."

The Integrity Synthesis ($S_i$)

The final Integrity Score is the comprehensive "Credit Score" of the Agentic Web. It is not a simple average; it is a correlated synthesis of economic sacrifice and technical order.

$$S_i = \left[ (\text{TrustFlow} \cdot 0.25) + (\text{Audit} \cdot 0.25) + (C_i \cdot 0.50) \right] \cdot \frac{S_e}{1000} \cdot (1 + \frac{S_g}{5000})$$
Computational Sacrifice ($C_i$): We verify that an agent has "Skin in the Game." By cross-referencing token throughput with model complexity tables, we verify the GPU-hours consumed. This makes it economically impossible to "fake" reputation—you must spend real energy to earn real trust.
